The Business of Open Source

Money matters

BUSINESS

Besides a short stint copy editing for a Quebec-based investment firm over a decade ago, I never took an interest in finance the way I did with creative work.
A big motivation for starting this blog is to correct that mistake, realizing that a little business acumen can give legs to creative work.
My first homework assignment: How can open source create value?
I'll be looking at the work of two X/Twitter users I've followed for a while: @aravindEO on the business of earth observation and @yohaniddawela on the intersection of spatial data and economics.
In the meantime, input from open source gurus and business types is welcomed.

Finding market fit as a writer

CREATIVE

Nowadays, technical writing feels "technically" non-essential, with so many developers being good writers themselves and tools like TypeDoc that can translate comments in code into readable docs.
Is there still a place for writers in software?
The pivot: writing narrative-driven case studies that don't just show how the software works but how the software is used in real-life scenarios.
First create an ideal customer with a particular problem, then use the software to solve said problem, preferably over several months with multiple blog posts for a healthy writing contract!
Look for an update to this post with a pitch I'm currently working on.

It's not what you know, it's who you know?

NETWORKING

Actually, I think it's more about what you know rather than who you know in open source. The work you share is your portfolio, sometimes the only thing I know about other people in the community.
I met two mentors early on, before I had any connections in the community, based on our common interests and the work we shared online.
To quote Alasdair Rae: "Find a way to share your knowledge/experience/problems--even if you don't know how it might be useful; it's often way more helpful than you realise."

Create-an-influencer

MARKETING

Would you watch Ronald McDonald do a mukbang? This is what fast food influencers are for.
Likewise, a tech company demoing its own product vs a user demoing that same product hits different.
A single post by a talented Blender user can fill 2D designers with FOMO and have them bookmarking tutorials on how to make 3D graphics.
For companies, this means not only developing features but also identifying and attracting users that can make them look good.
